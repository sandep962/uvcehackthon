How can I know if a natural disaster is happening or not?

  • Emergency Alerts: Sign up for emergency alerts from your local government or weather service.
  • Weather Apps: Use weather apps like Dark Sky, Weather Underground, or The Weather Channel.
  • News Reports: Monitor local news channels, radio stations, and online news sources.
  • Social Media: Follow official social media accounts from authorities and emergency management agencies.
  • Sirens: Pay attention to warning sirens in your area.
  • Nature: Observe changes in nature, such as unusual weather patterns, rising water levels, or increased earthquake activity.
  • Disaster Alert Apps: Download apps like FEMA, American Red Cross, or Earthquake Alert.
  • NOAA Weather Radio: Use a NOAA Weather Radio for emergency alerts and weather updates.
  • Local Authorities: Stay informed through local authorities, such as police, fire departments, or emergency management agencies.
  • Community Networks: Stay connected with your community through neighborhood networks or local organizations.

How can I know if a hurricane is happening or not?

  • National Hurricane Center (NHC) Alerts: Sign up for alerts from the NHC for updates on hurricane watches, warnings, and forecasts.
  • Hurricane Watch or Warning: Pay attention to local news and weather reports for hurricane watches or warnings issued by authorities.
  • Weather Apps: Use weather apps like Dark Sky, Weather Underground, or The Weather Channel for real-time updates on hurricane tracks and intensities.
  • Local News: Monitor local news channels, radio stations, and online news sources for updates on hurricane landfall, evacuations, and emergency instructions.
  • Sirens and Emergency Broadcasts: Pay attention to warning sirens and emergency broadcasts from local authorities.
  • Storm Surge Warnings: Be aware of storm surge warnings, which indicate potential coastal flooding.
  • Wind and Rain: Observe increasing winds and heavy rainfall, which can indicate a hurricane is approaching.
  • Pressure Drops: Monitor pressure drops, which can signal a hurricane's approach.
  • Hurricane Tracking Maps: Use online maps to track the hurricane's path and intensity.
  • Local Authorities: Stay informed through local authorities, such as emergency management agencies and law enforcement.

How can I know if an earthquake is happening or not?

  • Shaking or Tremors: Feel the ground shaking or trembling beneath your feet.
  • Earthquake Alert Apps: Use apps like ShakeAlert or Earthquake Alert for real-time notifications.
  • Sirens and Alarms: Hear warning sirens or alarms from local authorities or earthquake detection systems.
  • Noise or Rumbling: Hear a loud rumbling or noise, often described as a freight train or explosion.
  • Ground Cracking: See the ground cracking or opening up.
  • Objects Falling: See objects falling or toppling over.
  • Loss of Balance: Feel a loss of balance or difficulty standing.
  • Earthquake Early Warning Systems: Receive alerts from early warning systems, which detect seismic activity before shaking occurs.
  • Local News and Reports: Monitor local news and reports for earthquake updates and instructions.
  • Seismic Monitoring: Check online seismic monitoring websites or apps for earthquake activity.

How can I know if a flood is happening or not?

  • Rising Water Levels: Observe increasing water levels in rivers, streams, or coastal areas.
  • Flooding Warnings: Receive warnings from local authorities, emergency management agencies, or weather services.
  • Heavy Rainfall: Experience prolonged heavy rainfall or storms in your area.
  • Water Inundation: See water overflowing into streets, homes, or buildings.
  • Evacuation Orders: Receive evacuation orders from local authorities due to flooding.
  • Flood Alert Apps: Use apps like Flood Alert or Emergency Alert for real-time notifications.
  • Local News and Reports: Monitor local news and reports for flood updates and instructions.
  • Changes in Water Color or Smell: Notice unusual changes in water color, smell, or clarity.
  • Sirens and Alarms: Hear warning sirens or alarms from local authorities or flood detection systems.
  • Neighborhood Flooding: See flooding in neighboring areas or nearby communities.

How can I know if a wildfire is happening or not?

  • Smoke or Haze: See smoke or haze in the air, especially if it's thick and dark.
  • Fire Alerts: Receive alerts from local fire departments, emergency management agencies, or weather services.
  • Flames or Glow: Observe flames or a glow on the horizon or nearby areas.
  • Heat: Feel an increase in temperature or heat in your area.
  • Evacuation Orders: Receive evacuation orders from local authorities due to wildfire risks.
  • Wildfire Apps: Use apps like Wildfire Alert or Emergency Alert for real-time notifications.
  • Local News and Reports: Monitor local news and reports for wildfire updates and instructions.
  • Firefighters: See firefighters or emergency crews in the area responding to the wildfire.
  • Changes in Air Quality: Notice changes in air quality or breathing difficulties due to smoke.
  • Sirens and Alarms: Hear warning sirens or alarms from local authorities or wildfire detection systems.
